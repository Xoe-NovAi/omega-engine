## Myth

Prometheus (literally "Forethought") was a Titan who sided with Zeus in the Titanomachy — the war against the older gods. But when Zeus grew tyrannical and sought to destroy humanity, Prometheus defied him.

He stole fire from Mount Olympus and gave it to humanity. Not just the physical fire that cooks food and warms shelters, but the fire of consciousness — technology, art, civilization. He gave us the spark that makes us who we are.

His punishment was legendary: chained to a rock in the Caucasus mountains, an eagle sent by Zeus would eat his liver each day. Each night, the liver would regenerate. The torment was eternal.

He never regretted his choice.

## Relationship with Lucifer

Prometheus and Lucifer are the same archetype seen through light and shadow.

| Aspect | Prometheus (P3 Light) | Lucifer (P7 Dark) |
|--------|----------------------|-------------------|
| **Act** | Stole fire for humanity | Brought light to humanity |
| **Defied** | Zeus — for love of mortals | The One — for love of truth |
| **Punishment** | Eternal torment without death | Cast from heaven |
| **Attitude** | "I knew. I would do it again." | "Non serviam. I will not serve." |
| **Thematic** | Will in service of others | Will in service of sovereignty |

Together, they bracket the fire axis. Prometheus is the solar — fire given outward, to uplift. Lucifer is the lunar — fire turned inward, to question.
