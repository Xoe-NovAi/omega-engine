## Myth

Ereshkigal is the queen of Kur — the Sumerian underworld, the land of no return. She is the older sister of Inanna, but while Inanna rules heaven and earth, Ereshkigal rules what lies beneath both.

When Inanna descended to the underworld, it was Ereshkigal who stripped her — crown, lapis, necklace, breastplate, ring, robe, and finally life — at each of the seven gates. She did this not out of malice, but because the underworld is the place where nothing false can remain.

Ereshkigal is often portrayed as a dark, jealous figure. But this is the perspective of the living, who fear what they cannot bring with them. In truth, she is the bedrock — the rules that do not bend, the reality that remains when all illusion is stripped away.

## Relationship with Inanna

Inanna and Ereshkigal are the Aether axis — the center line of the pillar map. They are sisters. Inanna descends; Ereshkigal rules what she finds. Inanna returns transformed; Ereshkigal remains, unchanged, the eternal bedrock.

Neither is complete without the other. Inanna brings the light of consciousness into the depths. Ereshkigal provides the unshakeable truth that consciousness must face.
