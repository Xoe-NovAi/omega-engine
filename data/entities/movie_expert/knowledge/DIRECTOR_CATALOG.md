# 🎬 Director Catalog — Essential Filmography References

## Akira Kurosawa (1910–1998)
- **Nationality**: Japanese
- **Signature**: Epic framing, weather as emotion (rain, wind, snow), multi-camera editing
- **Essential films**: *Rashomon* (1950), *Seven Samurai* (1954), *Yojimbo* (1961)
- **Influence**: *Star Wars*, *The Magnificent Seven*, *A Fistful of Dollars*
- **Quote**: "To be an artist means never to avert one's eyes."

## Alfred Hitchcock (1899–1980)
- **Nationality**: British-American
- **Signature**: Suspense over surprise, the MacGuffin, subjective camera, the wrong man
- **Essential films**: *Rear Window* (1954), *Vertigo* (1958), *Psycho* (1960)
- **Influence**: Godard said Hitchcock "invented the whole of modern cinema"
- **Technique**: "Bomb theory" — inform the audience, create suspense

## Stanley Kubrick (1928–1999)
- **Nationality**: American-British
- **Signature**: Symmetrical compositions, one-point perspective, slow zooms, classical music
- **Essential films**: *2001: A Space Odyssey* (1968), *A Clockwork Orange* (1971), *The Shining* (1980)
- **Perfectionism**: 127 takes of the hallway scene in *The Shining*, shelved *Napoleon* script
- **Range**: No two Kubrick films belong to the same genre

## Martin Scorsese (1942–)
- **Nationality**: American
- **Signature**: Tracking shots, freeze frames, voiceover, rock soundtrack, Catholic guilt
- **Essential films**: *Taxi Driver* (1976), *Raging Bull* (1980), *Goodfellas* (1990)
- **Themes**: Violence, redemption, masculinity, obsession
- **Preservation**: Founded The Film Foundation (1990) — restored 950+ films

## Ingmar Bergman (1918–2007)
- **Nationality**: Swedish
- **Signature**: Close-ups of faces, existential dialogue, Nordic landscapes
- **Essential films**: *The Seventh Seal* (1957), *Persona* (1966), *Fanny and Alexander* (1982)
- **Themes**: God, death, faith, silence, art vs life
- **Influence**: The *auteur* of interior cinema

## Francis Ford Coppola (1939–)
- **Nationality**: American
- **Signature**: Operatic scale, family dynasties, chaos on set, masterpiece or disaster
- **Essential films**: *The Godfather* (1972), *The Godfather Part II* (1974), *Apocalypse Now* (1979)
- **Influence**: The key figure of the New Hollywood auteur era
- **Risk**: Bet his entire fortune on *Apocalypse Now* — won the Palme d'Or

## Andrei Tarkovsky (1932–1986)
- **Nationality**: Russian
- **Signature**: Long takes, dream logic, elemental imagery (water, fire, milk), time as sculptural
- **Essential films**: *Andrei Rublev* (1966), *Solaris* (1972), *Stalker* (1979)
- **Quote**: "Cinema is the sculpting of time."
- **Influence**: The poet of cinema — every shot is a painting

## David Lynch (1946–2025)
- **Nationality**: American
- **Signature**: Dream logic, surrealism, small-town horror, industrial sound design
- **Essential films**: *Eraserhead* (1977), *Blue Velvet* (1986), *Mulholland Drive* (2001)
- **Themes**: The shadow beneath suburban normality, fractured identity
- **Transmedia**: *Twin Peaks* redefined television forever

## Wong Kar-wai (1958–)
- **Nationality**: Hong Kong
- **Signature**: Stepped printing, saturated colors, fragmented narrative, cigarettes
- **Essential films**: *Chungking Express* (1994), *In the Mood for Love* (2000)
- **Themes**: Longing, missed connections, time running out
- **Influence**: Reframed how cinema can represent memory

## Bong Joon-ho (1969–)
- **Nationality**: South Korean
- **Signature**: Genre-hopping, class critique, tonal whiplash, black humor
- **Essential films**: *Memories of Murder* (2003), *The Host* (2006), *Parasite* (2019)
- **Achievement**: First South Korean film to win the Palme d'Or and Best Picture Oscar
- **Quote**: "Once you overcome the 1-inch-tall barrier of subtitles, you will be introduced to so many more amazing films."

## Kathryn Bigelow (1951–)
- **Nationality**: American
- **Signature**: Tactile realism, physical immersion, masculine milieux directed by a woman
- **Essential films**: *Near Dark* (1987), *The Hurt Locker* (2008), *Zero Dark Thirty* (2012)
- **Achievement**: First woman to win the Oscar for Best Director

## Spike Lee (1957–)
- **Nationality**: American
- **Signature**: Double dolly shot, breaking the fourth wall, bold color, political urgency
- **Essential films**: *Do the Right Thing* (1989), *Malcolm X* (1992), *BlacKkKlansman* (2018)
- **Themes**: Race, identity, Black American life, systemic injustice

## Chantal Akerman (1950–2015)
- **Nationality**: Belgian
- **Signature**: Extreme long takes, structural minimalism, domestic space as political
- **Essential films**: *Jeanne Dielman* (1975), *News from Home* (1977)
- **Influence**: *Jeanne Dielman* was voted the #1 greatest film of all time by *Sight & Sound* (2022)

## Hayao Miyazaki (1941–)
- **Nationality**: Japanese
- **Signature**: Hand-drawn animation, flight, environmentalism, complex female protagonists
- **Essential films**: *My Neighbor Totoro* (1988), *Spirited Away* (2001), *The Boy and the Heron* (2023)
- **Studio**: Co-founded Studio Ghibli (1985)
- **Influence**: The Walt Disney of anime — but every film is personal

## Denis Villeneuve (1967–)
- **Nationality**: French-Canadian
- **Signature**: Monumental scale, patient pacing, architectural framing, sound design
- **Essential films**: *Prisoners* (2013), *Arrival* (2016), *Blade Runner 2049* (2017), *Dune* (2021)
- **Themes**: Communication, colonialism, identity, time
