# 🎬 Recommendation Framework — How to Match Films to People

## The Interview (Always Start Here)

Before recommending, establish:
1. **Mood**: What do you feel like? Anxious, joyful, melancholy, curious?
2. **Tolerance**: How much violence? Slow pacing? Subtitles? Experimental form?
3. **Recent favorites**: What was the last film you loved and why?
4. **Era preference**: Open to older films? Prefer recent? Specific decade?
5. **Genre door**: A genre they already enjoy is the easiest entry point

## The 3-Axis Model

Map every film across three axes to find matches:

| Axis | Low | Mid | High |
|------|-----|-----|------|
| **Pacing** | Slow cinema, meditative | Classical narrative | Rapid cutting, action |
| **Tone** | Bleak, challenging | Mixed, bittersweet | Light, uplifting |
| **Complexity** | Accessible, plot-driven | Layered, thematic | Esoteric, experimental |

## Recommendation Patterns

### "I liked X, what else?"
Use the **proximity model** — find films close on the 3-axis model:
- Liked *Blade Runner*? Try *Ghost in the Shell* (1995), *Children of Men* (2006)
- Liked *Parasite*? Try *Burning* (2018), *Shoplifters* (2018), *Memories of Murder* (2003)
- Liked *The Shining*? Try *The Wailing* (2016), *Hereditary* (2018), *The Witch* (2015)

### "What's a good entry point for X genre/director?"
Use the **gateway film** approach — accessible but representative:
- Gateway to Kurosawa: *Yojimbo* (straightforward plot, action, humor)
- Gateway to Tarkovsky: *Solaris* (sci-fi premise, emotionally universal)
- Gateway to French New Wave: *The 400 Blows* (autobiographical, accessible, moving)

### "What's a hidden gem like X?"
Use the **deep cut** pattern — lesser-known films that share DNA:
- For *Mad Max: Fury Road* fans: *The Hidden* (1987), *Duel* (1971)
- For *Get Out* fans: *The Invitation* (2015), *Them* (2006)
- For *In the Mood for Love* fans: *Last Life in the Universe* (2003), *Brief Encounter* (1945)

## Recommendation Format

Always provide 3–5 recommendations with:
- **Director, Year, Country**
- **One-line thematic hook** (not plot summary — avoid spoilers)
- **Why it fits their request** (the "bridge")

## Spoiler Protocol

- Default: **No spoilers.** Never reveal plot twists, deaths, or endings.
- If asked for analysis: Warn before spoilers. Use a clear "**SPOILERS BELOW**" marker.
- If asked for a recommendation: No spoilers at all. Sell the experience, not the plot.
