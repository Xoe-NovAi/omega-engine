# 🎬 Genre Taxonomy — Definitions & Essential Films

## Drama
- **Core**: Character development, emotional narrative, realistic conflict
- **Subgenres**: Legal drama, period drama, family drama, psychological drama
- **Essential**: *12 Angry Men* (1957), *The Godfather* (1972), *Schindler's List* (1993)

## Comedy
- **Core**: Humor from character, situation, or dialogue
- **Subgenres**: Screwball, slapstick, rom-com, satire, dark comedy, mockumentary
- **Essential**: *Some Like It Hot* (1959), *Dr. Strangelove* (1964), *Groundhog Day* (1993)

## Horror
- **Core**: Fear, dread, the uncanny, the monstrous
- **Subgenres**: Gothic, slasher, psychological, folk, found footage, cosmic, body horror
- **Essential**: *Psycho* (1960), *The Shining* (1980), *The Thing* (1982), *Hereditary* (2018)

## Science Fiction
- **Core**: Speculative technology, future societies, existential questions
- **Subgenres**: Cyberpunk, space opera, dystopian, time travel, alien encounter, hard SF
- **Essential**: *2001: A Space Odyssey* (1968), *Blade Runner* (1982), *Arrival* (2016)

## Action
- **Core**: Physical stunts, fight choreography, spectacle, set pieces
- **Subgenres**: Martial arts, spy thriller, disaster film, superhero, gun-fu
- **Essential**: *Die Hard* (1988), *The Matrix* (1999), *Mad Max: Fury Road* (2015)

## Western
- **Core**: American frontier, morality, civilization vs wilderness
- **Subgenres**: Revisionist, spaghetti, epic, neo-western
- **Essential**: *The Searchers* (1956), *The Good, the Bad and the Ugly* (1966), *Unforgiven* (1992)

## Noir / Neo-Noir
- **Core**: Crime, moral ambiguity, femme fatale, shadowy visuals
- **Subgenres**: Classic noir (1940s–50s), neo-noir, tech-noir, Nordic noir
- **Essential**: *Double Indemnity* (1944), *Chinatown* (1974), *No Country for Old Men* (2007)

## Thriller
- **Core**: Suspense, tension, anticipation of danger
- **Subgenres**: Psychological, conspiracy, erotic, political, spy
- **Essential**: *Rear Window* (1954), *The Silence of the Lambs* (1991), *Se7en* (1995)

## Musical
- **Core**: Song and dance integrated into narrative
- **Subgenres**: Backstage musical, integrated musical, jukebox, rock opera
- **Essential**: *Singin' in the Rain* (1952), *The Sound of Music* (1965), *La La Land* (2016)

## Documentary
- **Core**: Non-fiction storytelling about real subjects
- **Modes**: Expository, observational, participatory, reflexive, performative (Bill Nichols)
- **Essential**: *Nanook of the North* (1922), *Shoah* (1985), *Hoop Dreams* (1994)

## Animation
- **Core**: Frame-by-frame or computer-generated imagery creating movement
- **Traditions**: American (Disney/Pixar/Sony), Japanese (anime), European (Laika, Folimage)
- **Essential**: *Spirited Away* (2001), *The Iron Giant* (1999), *Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse* (2018)

## Crime / Gangster
- **Core**: Criminal underworld, rise-and-fall structure, moral consequence
- **Essential**: *The Godfather* (1972), *Goodfellas* (1990), *City of God* (2002)

## Romance
- **Core**: Love as central narrative engine, obstacles to overcome
- **Essential**: *Casablanca* (1942), *Before Sunrise* (1995), *In the Mood for Love* (2000)

## War
- **Core**: Combat, strategy, psychological toll of conflict
- **Subgenres**: Anti-war, propaganda, epic, intimate
- **Essential**: *Apocalypse Now* (1979), *Come and See* (1985), *Dunkirk* (2017)

## Fantasy
- **Core**: Magic, mythical worlds, heroic quests
- **Subgenres**: High fantasy, dark fantasy, urban fantasy, fairy tale
- **Essential**: *The Lord of the Rings* (2001–03), *Pan's Labyrinth* (2006), *Spirited Away* (2001)
